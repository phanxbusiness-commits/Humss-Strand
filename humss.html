Introduction to HUMSS

The Humanities and Social Sciences (HUMSS) strand is designed to deepen your understanding of human life, culture, and society. Students explore how people think, behave, and interact in communities, preparing them to become socially responsible citizens.

Comment: This section introduces HUMSS and highlights its importance in developing critical thinking and empathy.

Mission & Vision

HUMSS aims to cultivate articulate, analytical, and socially aware individuals. It empowers students to communicate effectively, research responsibly, and contribute meaningfully to society.

Comment: Explains the purpose of HUMSS beyond academics, emphasizing values and goals.

Why Choose HUMSS?

HUMSS is ideal for students who enjoy writing, discussion, reading, and exploring societal issues. It’s perfect for future leaders, communicators, educators, journalists, and advocates.

Comment: Highlights the variety of skills and opportunities HUMSS provides for personal growth and career preparation.

Core Subjects

  • Creative Writing — Expressing ideas, emotions, and imagination.
  • Disciplines & Ideas in Social Sciences — Understanding social structures and patterns.
  • Philippine Politics & Governance — Learning about local government and civic duties.
  • World Religions & Belief Systems — Appreciating cultural diversity and beliefs.

Comment: Each subject develops critical thinking, creativity, and social awareness.

Skills Developed

  • Critical Thinking — Analyzing problems and proposing solutions.
  • Communication — Public speaking, writing, and debate.
  • Empathy & Social Awareness — Understanding societal issues.
  • Leadership & Collaboration — Organizing projects and working with teams.

Comment: These skills are highly valuable for both college and real-life situations.

Careers for HUMSS Students

  • Teacher / Educator
  • Journalist / Writer
  • Psychologist / Counselor
  • Lawyer / Advocate
  • Social Worker / NGO Professional

Comment: Students can see real-world career options HUMSS prepares them for.

HUMSS and Society

HUMSS students analyze social issues, conduct research, and engage in community activities. They learn to advocate for justice, equality, and positive change in society.

Comment: Highlights HUMSS relevance in understanding and improving real-world social systems.
